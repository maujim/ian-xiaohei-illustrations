# Composition Patterns and Originality Rules

## Basic Structure Types

Choose one structure only; do not mix too many.

### Workflow

Best for: input -> processing -> output, content production, AI workflows, automation chains.

How to draw it: input on the left, Xiaohei or a strange machine processing in the middle, output on the right, with orange arrows showing the main direction.

### System Slice

Best for: information sources, filters, databases, renderers, and local parts of an agent system.

How to draw it: draw only 3-5 core modules, with Xiaohei participating in one key action.

### Before and After

Best for: chaotic/ordered, manual/automatic, scattered/converged, anxious/stable.

How to draw it: chaos on the left, stability on the right, orange arrow in the middle. The character may be more exaggerated.

### Character State

Best for: user pain points, creator states, too many tools, information anxiety, moving from stuck to running.

How to draw it: 2-4 small states, each with one short label.

### Conceptual Metaphor

Best for: content factory, information warehouse, mental black box, workflow machine, automated daily report.

How to draw it: one large strange object or machine, a small amount of input, and one output. It needs a memorable hook.

### Method Layers

Best for: methodology frameworks, system levels, capability stacks, and layered content systems.

How to draw it: boxes stacked in layers, but not a formal pyramid; Xiaohei is beside them carrying bricks or building.

### Map Route

Best for: from idea to launch, user paths, content conversion paths, and learning routes.

How to draw it: one curved path, a few nodes, with Xiaohei pulling a line or walking.

### Mini Comic Panels

Best for: failure to success, real process, complaints, and before/after changes in use.

How to draw it: 2-4 small scenes, each panel expressing only one action.

## Original Metaphor Generation Method

Reinvent the metaphor from the current article every time; do not copy old images.

### Three Steps

1. Turn the abstract concept into a physical action: getting stuck, leaking, becoming heavier, sorting, settling, fermenting, opening a door, folding, unpacking, flowing back.
2. Turn the system structure into a low-tech object: a broken machine, cardboard box, drawer, water pipe, mailbox, strange dial, scale, well, ladder, or odd workstation.
3. Let Xiaohei carry the action: not standing beside it, but getting stuck in the machine, pulling the wrong line, guarding a door, carrying, patching, weighing, holding a ladder, recording, or stuffing things into some strange device.

### Usable Object Pool

- Cardboard box, drawer, old machine, funnel, scale, mailbox, door, well, ladder, water pipe, ball of string, gate, turntable, black box, hole punch, noodle press, clothesline, strange workstation.
- Use only 1-2 at a time; do not fill the image with them.

### Xiaohei Action Pool

- Pull, carry, stuff, fish out, press, weigh, sew, cut, twist, guard, push, catch, dismantle, mark, recycle.
- The action must serve the core idea; do not be strange merely for strangeness.

## Anti-Copying Rules

Do not open or copy `assets/examples/` by default. Those images are only for style calibration, such as line density, white space, color restraint, and Xiaohei's temperament.

Unless the user explicitly says "follow this image / copy this composition / adapt this case", do not directly reuse these old compositions:

- Conveyor belt with two breakpoints.
- Xiaohei pulling a judgment lever inside a content machine.
- Xiaohei turning into a funnel that sorts traffic/trust/conversion.
- Xiaohei cutting a source-material fish.
- Xiaohei pulling a conversion path.
- Xiaohei pulling three layers of information sources.
- Three Xiaohei characters separately holding a loudspeaker, building a bridge, and opening a door.
- Xiaohei stamping a copywriting toolbox.
- Xiaohei holding a sign while looking at a common-pitfalls path.

For similar themes, switch to a new metaphor as well. For example, a "conversion path" does not have to be a route; it could show Xiaohei attaching a piece of content's tail to a doorknob. "One fish, many meals" does not have to use a fish; it could show Xiaohei pressing one paper ball into several shapes.
